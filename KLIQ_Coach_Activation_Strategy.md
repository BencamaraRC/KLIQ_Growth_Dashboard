# PART 2: INDUSTRY RESEARCH â€” What the Best Platforms Do

## 2.1 â€” Kajabi Case Study: "First Sale in 90 Days"

**Kajabi** (the leading creator platform, $500M funding round) faced the exact same problem: coaches signing up and churning during trial.

**Their data-driven solution:**
- Identified that **creators who achieved their first sale within 90 days** were significantly more likely to stay
- Rebuilt onboarding around **getting to first sale**, not just "setting up your account"
- Broke down essential steps: account setup â†’ customer success engagement â†’ free coaching calls â†’ course setup
- Aligned messaging across the entire journey (website â†’ trial â†’ post-trial)
- **Result:** Significantly higher trial conversion rate, millions in additional revenue

**KLIQ parallel:** Our data shows blog engagement (+43pp) and community posting (+27pp) are the strongest retention signals. These are proxies for "creating value for users" â€” the equivalent of Kajabi's "first sale."

**Recommendation for KLIQ:** Reframe the entire onboarding around **"Get your first user engaged"** rather than "Set up your store."

## 2.2 â€” The "Aha Moment" Framework (ProductLed)

The best SaaS companies identify their **activation event** â€” the single action that, once completed, makes a user dramatically more likely to retain.

| Company | Aha Moment | Impact |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Slack | 2,000 messages sent | Retention jumps from 30% to 93% |
| Dropbox | 1 file uploaded | 2x retention |
| Facebook | 7 friends in 10 days | 3x retention |
| Hubspot | Using 5 features | 2.5x retention |
| **KLIQ** | **Blog engagement in Week 1** | **50.8% vs 7.5% retention (6.8x)** |

**KLIQ's Aha Moment = Blog engagement in Week 1.** The entire onboarding should be engineered to get coaches to this point.

## 2.3 â€” Gamification That Works (Industry Data)

Research from Userpilot, StriveCloud, and others shows:

- **Onboarding checklists** â†’ 40% increase in activation rates (Talana case study)
- **Gamified welcome screens** â†’ 47% increase in activation (Attention Insight case study)
- **Progress bars** â†’ Leverage the Zeigarnik Effect (people feel compelled to complete unfinished tasks)
- **Milestone celebrations** â†’ Asana's unicorn animation, Duolingo's streaks
- **70% of Global 2000 companies** now use gamification in onboarding
- Companies using gamification see **up to 50% reduction in early churn**

**Key principles:**
1. **3-5 tasks max** in the checklist (not 12)
2. **Start at 20% complete** (pre-fill something so it doesn't feel empty)
3. **Celebrate every completion** (confetti, sound, encouraging message)
4. **Show social proof** ("87% of successful coaches completed this step")

## 2.4 â€” The "Empty State" Problem (ProductLed)

> "It's disheartening for new users to see a bunch of zeros and placeholder images on the main page."

**KLIQ's current problem:** After onboarding, coaches face a blank dashboard. No content, no users, no revenue â€” just zeros.

**Best practice solutions:**
- **Pre-populate with sample content** (Basecamp shows dummy projects to teach the interface)
- **Show what success looks like** ("Here's what [Top Coach]'s dashboard looks like after 30 days")
- **Direct to immediate action** (Dropbox Paper: "Start by creating your first document")
- **Never show zeros** â€” show progress toward first milestone instead

## 2.5 â€” The "Golden Window" (ProductFruits)

> "Send a friendly in-app banner and a concise email within five minutes of sign-up. This 'golden window' is when curiosity is highest and distractions are few."

**KLIQ data confirms this:** 12.3% of coaches who take action do so in the first 15 minutes. After 4 hours, the probability of ever taking action drops dramatically.

**Seven tactics from ProductFruits:**
1. **Automate welcomes** â€” within 5 minutes, reference the user's goal
2. **Personalise tours** â€” detect device, plan tier, marketing channel
3. **Embed AI self-support** â€” answer questions without leaving the screen
4. **Highlight quick wins** â€” first meaningful victory in 5 clicks
5. **Progressive disclosure** â€” hide complexity until needed
6. **Solicit early feedback** â€” one-question survey after first value event
7. **Iterate weekly** â€” Monday metrics, Tuesday hypotheses, Wednesday A/B test
